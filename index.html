Week 1 News

FJWU Hosts Panel on Gender-Based Violence: Rawalpindi—Fatima Jinnah Women University (FJWU) held a panel discussion as part of the 16 Days of Activism Against Gender-Based Violence. Organized by the Department of Gender Studies and ORIC in collaboration with NCA Pakistan, ROZAN, and NCSW, the event focused on the theme, “From Peace at Home to Peace in the World.” Key speakers included NCSW Chairperson Ms. Umm e Laila Azhar and experts from academia and advocacy. Vice Chancellor Dr. Bushra Mirza reaffirmed FJWU’s commitment to a violence-free society in her closing remarks. The discussion highlighted collective action to combat gender-based violence.

Rosa Park Day: Honoring Courage and Legacy: Lincoln Corners Pakistan hosted Rosa Park Day at SBARR. This event focused on the theme "Sit Down for Injustice, Stand Up for Change," celebrating Rosa Parks' pivotal role in the civil rights movement and inspiring attendees to reflect on the ongoing struggle for social justice. Participants engaged in meaningful discussions about courage and legacy, emphasizing the importance of standing against injustice in today's society.

Human Library Session: Dr. Noor ul Huda, a Ph.D. holder with a visual disability, led a Human Library session organized by the FJWU Library Society on 3rd December 2024. This event aimed to foster understanding and empathy by allowing participants to engage with Dr. Huda's personal experiences and insights, highlighting the importance of inclusivity and diverse perspectives in academia.

Grand Gala 2024: The Department of Bioinformatics & Biotechnology hosted its annual Grand Gala 2024, celebrating the achievements of students and faculty. This vibrant event, held on Campus II, provided attendees with a memorable experience filled with delicious food displays and engaging moments.

Times Consultant Event: Times Consultant aimed at exploring study abroad opportunities in countries. Providing students with essential insights into course selection, visa processes, and university applications. This event offered a unique platform for students to engage directly with consultants and gain valuable guidance for their educational journeys abroad.

Alumni Corner: I'm thrilled to share that I participated in an Inter-University thesis competition, where I competed against 24-25 MS and PhD holders. As one of the only BS students from Fatima Jinnah, I'm proud to announce that two of our students secured positions in the competition! Suman Riza secured the third position and received a cash prize, along with a certificate and a trophy, for her outstanding research on "The Impact of Climate Change on Women's Well-being." Another student from our university secured the second position.
